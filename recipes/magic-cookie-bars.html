Magic Cookie Bars

Description

Magic cookie bars are a delightfully rich dessert that is actually quick and easy to make. No blender or fancy equipment needed. Dump melted butter, graham cracker crumbs, walnuts, chocolate chips shredded coconut, and condensed milk in a baking dish and bake. No need for mixing, blending, or six thousand different bowls. Once you have made these delectable sweets a couple times you won't even need to measure, the recipe is very forgiving. Don't you love when a simple recipe is actually simple?

The walnuts and coconut may seem like a hard sell for those pickier folks, but douse anything in enough melted butter and condensed milk (basically just liquid sugar) and most people will eat just about anything. This recipe can easily be made dairy free by swapping butter for dairy-free margaraine sticks and using condensed coconut or oat milk in place of the condensed dairy milk. To elimnate walnuts from the recipe, swap them for something like hulled sunflower seeds or pretzels. Also, if you can't find graham crackers, the crumbs of just about any crunchy cookie can be utilized.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup melted butter (or margaraine)
  • 1 1/2 graham cracker crumbs
  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 1 1/3 shredded coconut
  • 14 oz can condensed milk (or condensed coconut/oat milk)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 farenheit.
  2. Melt 2 sticks or 1 cup of butter (or margaraine) in the microwave or a sauce pan on the stove. While the butter melts, crush about 1 1/2 cups of graham crackers.
  3. Into a 9 x 13-inch pan, pour your melted butter. Then, in this order, sprinkle the graham cracker crumbs, walnuts, chocolate chips, and shredded coconut. Evenly drizzle the condensed milk over the entire dish.
  4. Bake for 25 minutes. Cool for 15 minutes. Chill for an hour before cutting.
